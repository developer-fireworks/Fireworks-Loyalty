# Sales by Brands

## Overview

Analyze sales performance across different tenant brands within the loyalty program network. This report helps track which brands are driving the most transactions, revenue, and member engagement, enabling data-driven decisions for tenant management and partnership strategies.

## Purpose

The Sales by Brands report enables you to:
- Monitor individual brand/tenant performance
- Compare sales across participating merchants
- Identify top-performing brands
- Track brand contribution to overall program success
- Make informed tenant partnership decisions
- Optimize tenant mix and mall space allocation
- Support tenant relations with performance data

## Features

- **Brand Performance Tracking**: Monitor sales by individual brands/tenants
- **Visual Analytics**: Charts and graphs for easy interpretation
- **Multi-Tenant Support**: Track all participating brands in one view
- **Comparative Analysis**: Compare performance across brands
- **Trend Identification**: Spot patterns in brand performance
- **Revenue Insights**: Understand which brands drive the most value

## Key Metrics Explained

### Primary Metrics

| Metric | Description | Use Case |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Transaction Count** | Total number of purchases | Volume indicator |
| **Revenue** | Total sales value | Financial performance |
| **Average Transaction Value** | Revenue ÷ Transactions | Customer spending behavior |
| **Market Share** | Brand % of total sales | Competitive position |
| **Growth Rate** | Period-over-period change | Trend analysis |

## Analysis Techniques

### Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)

**Identify Key Contributors:**
```
Analysis: Which brands drive 80% of revenue?

Results:
- Brand A: 32% of revenue (Top contributor)
- Brand B: 27% of revenue
- Brand C: 23% of revenue
Total: 82% from top 3 brands

Insight: 3 brands (out of 15) = 82% of revenue
Strategy: Protect these key relationships
Action: Premium support, contract security
```

### Trend Analysis

**Period-over-Period Comparison:**
```
Brand A Performance:
- Jan: RM 40,000
- Feb: RM 42,000 (+5%)
- Mar: RM 45,000 (+7%)
- Trend: Accelerating growth ✓

Brand D Performance:
- Jan: RM 30,000
- Feb: RM 28,000 (-7%)
- Mar: RM 25,500 (-9%)
- Trend: Declining ⚠️

Actions:
- Brand A: Explore expansion opportunities
- Brand D: Urgent intervention required
```

### Benchmark Analysis

**Against Category Averages:**
```
Fashion Category Average:
- Transactions: 240/month
- Revenue: RM 36,000/month
- Avg Value: RM 150

Brand A (Fashion):
- Transactions: 450/month (+88% vs avg)
- Revenue: RM 45,000/month (+25% vs avg)
- Avg Value: RM 100 (-33% vs avg)

Insight: High volume, competitive pricing
Strategy: Volume leader, maintain pricing
```

### Correlation Analysis

**Brand Relationships:**
```
Positive Correlation:
- Brand A (Fashion) ↑ = Brand B (Accessories) ↑
- Synergy: 0.75 correlation coefficient
- Action: Cross-promote, bundle offers

Negative Correlation:
- Brand C (Premium Restaurant) ↑ = Brand D (Food Court) ↓
- Competition: -0.60 correlation
- Action: Differentiate positioning
```

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Problem: Brand not appearing in report**

Solutions:
1. Verify brand has transactions in period
2. Check brand is active in system
3. Confirm brand properly tagged
4. Review data sync status
5. Contact technical support

**Problem: Data seems incorrect**

Solutions:
1. Verify date range settings
2. Check for recent data imports
3. Cross-reference with other reports
4. Review transaction categorization
5. Validate brand mappings

**Problem: Unable to compare periods**

Solutions:
1. Ensure consistent date ranges
2. Account for calendar differences
3. Consider seasonal factors
4. Check for data completeness
5. Use percentage metrics not absolutes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How is a "brand" defined?**
A: A brand represents a tenant merchant or business partner participating in the loyalty program.

**Q: Can I see individual product performance?**
A: This depends on system configuration. This report focuses on brand-level aggregation.

**Q: How do I add a new brand to tracking?**
A: Contact your system administrator to register new brands in the system.

**Q: Can I customize the date range?**
A: Yes, if custom date filters are available in your system configuration.

**Q: Why do percentages not add to 100%?**
A: Small brands may be grouped in "Others" category, or rounding may cause minor discrepancies.

**Q: How can I get brand-specific reports?**
A: Use filters (if available) or export data for custom analysis.
